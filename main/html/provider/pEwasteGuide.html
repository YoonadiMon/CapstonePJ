How to Prepare Your E-Waste

Follow these simple steps to ensure your e-waste is ready for collection or drop-off.

1
Backup Your Data
Before disposing of any device that stores information (phones, laptops, computers), make sure to back up your important files and photos.
5
Pack Securely
Place items in a box or bag to prevent damage during collection. For fragile items like monitors, use padding if available.
2
Wipe Personal Information
Perform a factory reset on phones and computers, or use data wiping software to protect your privacy. Remove SIM and memory cards.
4
Do Not Dismantle
Leave devices intact. Professional recyclers have the right equipment to safely separate materials.
3
Remove Batteries (If Possible)
For safety during transport, remove batteries if they are detachable. For batteries that are built-in, leave them in place.

What Happens to Your E-Waste?

Ever wondered what happens after your e-waste is disposed of? The journey ...

Step 1: Collection & Sorting

E-waste arrives at the recycling facility and is sorted by type (e.g., computers, phones, appliances).

Step 2: Manual Disassembly

Trained workers carefully take apart devices by hand, separating different components:

  • Circuit boards (contain precious metals)
  • Plastic casings
  • Glass (from monitors and screens)
  • Metals (steel, aluminum, copper)
  • Batteries (sent for specialized recycling)

Step 3: Shredding & Separation

Materials are shredded into small pieces. Magnets, eddy currents, and other technologies separate different materials:

  • Ferrous metals (iron, steel) are pulled out by magnets
  • Non-ferrous metals (aluminum, copper) are separated by eddy currents
  • Plastics are sorted by type

Step 4: Material Recovery

Separated materials are sent to specialized facilities:

  • Metals are melted down and reused in manufacturing
  • Plastics are processed into pellets for new products
  • Precious metals (gold, silver, copper) are extracted from circuit boards

Step 5: Safe Disposal

Hazardous materials that cannot be recycled (like certain chemicals) are treated and disposed of safely according to environmental regulations.
